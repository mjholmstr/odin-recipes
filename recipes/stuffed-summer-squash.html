Shrimp and Veggie Stuffed Yellow Summer Squash

Crooknech squash is great to use for this recipe , if available, because it offers a wide cavity to be filled with the delicious stuffing. This recipe os from the June/July 2021 issue of allrecipes magazine.

Ingredients

  • 2 (8-oz.) yellow summer squash
  • 4 Tbsp. olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1/2 lb large shrimp, shelled, deveined, and cut in half
  • 1 large tomato, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 8 cremini mushrooms, quartered
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 8 leaves fresh basil, torn
  • 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp. black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp. garlic powder

Steps

  1. Arrange oven rack 6 inches from heat source and preheat broiler. Grease a baking sheet.
  2. Cut squash in half lengthwise and scoop out seeds and enough pulp to leave 1/2 inch thick shell. Brush halves with 1 Tbsp. oil and arrange, cut sides down, on prepared baking sheet. Broil squash until hot and beginning to release beads of moisture, about 5 minutes. Remove squash from oven and adjust heat to 450F.
  3. Heat 2 Tbsp. oil in large skillet over medium-low heat. Add chopped garlic and shallot. Cook, stirring frequently, until shallot is translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Toss together remaining 1 Tbsp. oil, shrimp, tomato, mushrooms, 1/4 cup Parmesan, basil, cooked garlic and shallot, salt, pepper, and garlic powder in a bowl. Spoon shrimp mixture into squash halves and sprinkle with remaining 1/4 cup Parmesan.
  5. Bake stuffed squash until cheese is browned, filling is hot, and shrimp are opaque, about 20 minutes.
